# Coffee Roasting Guide

Roasting transforms green coffee beans into the aromatic brown beans we know and love.

## Roast Levels

### Light Roast

- **Temperature:** 356°F - 401°F (180°C - 205°C)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Light brown color
  - No oil on surface
  - High acidity
  - Origin flavors prominent
- **Common names:** Cinnamon, New England, Half City

### Medium Roast

- **Temperature:** 410°F - 428°F (210°C - 220°C)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Medium brown color
  - Balanced flavor
  - Medium acidity and body
- **Common names:** City, American, Breakfast

### Dark Roast

- **Temperature:** 437°F - 446°F (225°C - 230°C)
- **Characteristics:**
  - Dark brown to nearly black
  - Oil visible on surface
  - Low acidity
  - Bittersweet, smoky flavors
- **Common names:** French, Italian, Spanish

## The Roasting Process

### Stages

1. **Drying Phase** (0-4 minutes)
   - Moisture evaporates
   - Beans turn yellow

2. **Maillard Phase** (4-8 minutes)
   - Sugars and amino acids react
   - Browning begins
   - Aromas develop

3. **First Crack** (8-10 minutes)
   - Audible popping sound
   - Beans expand
   - Light roast complete

4. **Second Crack** (12-14 minutes)
   - Quieter cracking
   - Cell structure breaks down
   - Dark roast territory

## Home Roasting

### Equipment Options

- **Popcorn Popper** - Budget option, limited control
- **Drum Roaster** - Even roasting, larger batches
- **Air Roaster** - Fast, consistent results

### Cooling

Immediately cool beans after roasting to stop the process:
- Colander method
- Dedicated cooling tray
- Fan cooling

## Resting Period

Coffee needs to degas after roasting:
- **Light roasts:** 3-7 days
- **Dark roasts:** 7-14 days

Peak flavor typically occurs 1-2 weeks post-roast.
